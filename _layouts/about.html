About Dalia Katan

Dalia is a strategist, designer, entrepreneur, and public speaker who uses human-centered design to unlock the potential of organizations and humans alike.

Dalia previously consulted with Doblin Innovation and Deloitte where she worked hand-in-hand with management at Fortune-500 companies to design strategies that engage consumers, transform organizations and teams, and drive meaningful growth. Dalia spearheaded Doblin Innovation's expansion to the West Coast and was also selected for an innovation fellowship with the Center for the Edge in 2016. Prior to Deloitte, Dalia led marketing and user experience at several e-commerce and media technology startups, and held internships at IBM and UBS. From 2007-2011, she owned a graphic and web design business that served over 4M annual users.

Dalia's passion projects center on human connection and bridging divides: Dalia co-founded Deloitte's Refugee Community of Interest and launched an offering on refugee integration in the workplace, backed by a $400K grant from Deloitte leadership. She also interned at the U.S. House of Representatives from 2008-2010.
