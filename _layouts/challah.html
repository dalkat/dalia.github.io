Challah Bake

Servings
Makes 2 medium challahs, or one large challah

Kitchen Items
  • Mixing bowl
  • Wooden spoon (bulk here)
  • Baking tray
  • Kitchen towel
  • Pastry brush (like this)

Ingredients
  • 1 packet of Active Dry Yeast (thiskind)
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 whole egg
  • 1 egg yolk (for coating)
  • 1.5 tsp. salt
  • 4-6 cups of all-purpose flour (you will start with 4)
  • Optional: 1.5 cups chocolate chips or raisins
  • Optional: sesame seeds, poppy seeds, or sprinkles
Many thanks to Chabad SF for inspiring the recipe!
Instructions
  1. Place yeast in mixing bowl and add in warm water and sugar. Mix and allow to stand for five minutes - yeast should produce some bubbles in the water.
  2. Beat the egg in a small cup, then mix in egg, oil, and salt with rest of mixture.
  3. Gradually add the flour, 1/2 cup at a time, mixing well after each addition to avoid clumps of flower. As mixture thickens, begin kneading the dough. Continue adding flour and kneading dough until dough is smooth and soft, and no longer sticky (but not too dry either).
  4. Optional: If adding chocolate chips or raisins, work into dough as you add the flour in the previous step.
  5. Cover bowl with slightly damp kitchen towel and let the dough rise for an hour.
  6. Once the dough has risen, split the dough into three equal parts. Lightly sprinkle flour onto a clear surface or cutting board and roll the three parts into logs. Then braid the challah.
  7. Cover a baking tray with foil and lightly flour or oil the surface. Set the braided challah dough on the baking tray and set it aside for 30-60 minutes to continue rising (option to cover with towel).
  8. Pre-heat oven to 350 degrees. Brush egg yolk onto the top of the challah, and optionally sprinkle with sesame/poppy seeds or sprinkles! Bake for 30 minutes or until golden brown. Enjoy!
