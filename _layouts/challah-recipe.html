Challah Bake

Servings
Makes 2 medium challahs, or one large challah

Kitchen Items
  • Mixing bowl
  • Wooden spoon
  • Foil-lined baking tray
  • Kitchen towel
  • Pastry brush
  • Optional: disposal gloves to keep hands dough-free

Ingredients
  • 1 packet of Active Dry Yeast
  • 1 cup lukewarm water
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 whole egg
  • 1 egg yolk (for coating/egg wash)
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 4-5 cups of all-purpose flour (start with 4 cups)
  • Optional: 1 cup chocolate chips or raisins
  • Optional: sesame seeds, poppy seeds, or sprinkles
Many thanks to Chabad SF for inspiring the recipe!
Instructions
    Make the dough
  1. Combine yeast, sugar, and lukewarm water in mixing bowl. Mix and allow to stand for 5-10 minutes (yeast should bubble or foam).
  2. Beat the egg in a small cup, then mix in egg, oil, and salt with rest of mixture.
  3. Gradually add the flour, 1/2 cup at a time, mixing well after each addition to avoid clumps (you can knead them out later though!). As mixture thickens, begin kneading the dough as you add flour. Knead dough for 5-10 minutes until dough is smooth and soft, continuing to add flour as needed until the dough is no longer too sticky.
  4. Optional: If adding chocolate chips or raisins, work into dough as you add the flour in the previous step.
  5. Shape the dough into a ball and leave in the mixing bowl. Cover bowl with slightly damp kitchen towel and let the dough rise for an 1-2 hours, punch it down to remove air, then let it rise again for another 30 minutes to an hour. Do not knead.

    6. Braid and bake!
  6. Once the dough has risen, split the dough into four equal parts for a four-braid challah. Roll into logs on a lightly floured surface or cutting board, and braid.
  7. Pre-heat oven to 350 degrees. Line a baking tray with foil and lightly flour or oil the surface. Set the braided challah dough on the tray and set it aside in a warm place for 30-60 minutes to continue rising (option to cover with towel).
  8. Brush the challah with egg yolk (could mix with a teaspoon of water to loosen up the yolk). Optionally sprinkle with sesame/poppy seeds or sprinkles! Bake for 30 minutes or until golden brown. Note that the challah will expand as it bakes, so make sure it has at least 3 inches of space on either side to grow.
  9. Enjoy!
